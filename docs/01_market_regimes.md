# Market Regimes

Financial markets do not behave as stationary processes.

Instead they transition between different **regimes** characterized by changes in:

- volatility
- liquidity
- trend persistence
- mean reversion
- order flow pressure

Examples include:

| Regime | Characteristics |
|------|------|
| Trending | persistent directional moves |
| Range-bound | mean reverting |
| Volatility expansion | large moves |
| Volatility compression | quiet markets |

---

## Why Regimes Matter

Strategies behave differently across regimes.

Example:

Trend following performs well when

trend persistence > noise

Mean reversion performs well when

noise > directional movement
